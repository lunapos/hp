When you're choosing a POS system, what are you really looking for?

Slips get printed. Bills get settled. Sales get tracked. — Honestly, any service can do that. There are differences in UI preferences and pricing, but no one's ever said "we literally can't operate without this specific POS."

So why did we go out of our way to build a new one?

## POS Has Become a Commodity

Slip management, billing, sales reports. Whichever POS you install, the basics are the same.

There are one-time purchase models and cloud-based ones. Some cost hundreds of thousands of yen upfront; others charge monthly. Line up the features side by side, and no decisive difference emerges.

**POS is a commodity now.**

That's why Luna Pos doesn't aim to "win on POS features." Of course, we're confident in the usability we've built natively with SwiftUI — a UI you can figure out without hand-holding support. That's table stakes. But it's just the foundation.

What we're focused on is **what happens with the data** that accumulates in your POS day after day.

## Data Becomes "Store Credibility"

Investment money does flow into the nightlife industry. Funding from acquaintances, connections within the industry, investments from regulars, individual investors — money moves through these channels. That's a fact.

But the sources of capital are far too limited.

Banks are generally reluctant to lend to nightlife businesses. VCs and funds don't know how to access the market. Foreign investors might be interested in Japan's nightlife scene, but there's no way for information to even reach them. In practice, the industry runs on "insider money."

Why doesn't it expand outward? **Because investors don't have the information they need to make decisions.**

In the restaurant industry, there's sales trajectory, average spend per customer, cost ratios, repeat rates — the data exists, and it's used to make lending and investment decisions. But in the nightlife industry, almost no venue systematically accumulates this kind of data. Even if bookkeeping exists, it's not granular enough for investment decisions.

In other words, it's not that the market lacks appeal — **there's just no way to present it.** We believe there's enormous room for growth here.

### Luna Pos Fund (In Development on Our Roadmap)

What Luna Pos is developing is **a funding and investment-matching system for store owners**, powered by the sales data accumulated in the POS.

Here's how it works.

Stores that use Luna Pos every day continuously accumulate real-time data — daily sales, average spend, customer count, repeat rates. With the owner's consent, this data can be made visible to investors.

For investors, what was once an "opaque box" — a nightlife business with no visibility into its operations — becomes readable through numbers. Beyond insider funding, VCs, funds, and foreign investors — sources of capital that were previously inaccessible — can now connect through data.

**A store running Luna Pos = a store investors can evaluate with data = a store that can attract capital from both domestic and international sources.**

When this structure is in place, owners who were limited to expanding only through their own revenue will have new options. For staff too, having "data to back you up" becomes a real advantage when going independent or launching a new venue.

## Data Becomes "Career Assets for Hostesses"

There's another future we're building toward.

Hostesses have customers. Regulars who nominate them, clients they've built trust with over time. That's undeniably a major asset.

But **there's no data to back it up.**

When moving to a new club, or doing a trial shift, the only way to prove your ability is to talk about it. In a short interview, can you really convey years of hospitality skill? You might quote your sales numbers, but strengths like "I'm great at in-store nominations," "I can shift the energy of a table when I'm on assist," or "My repeat rate is high" — these can't be communicated through words alone.

Let's be honest — even in regular day jobs, there's no way to verify whether the numbers on a resume are real. But in this industry, where embellishment is part of the culture, self-reported claims carry even less weight. That's exactly why having verifiable proof creates an immediate edge.

### Luna Pos Career (In Development on Our Roadmap)

What Luna Pos is developing is **a career support system** powered by the performance data accumulated in the POS.

What we want to emphasize here is that **this is not a system where only the top sellers get recognized.**

Hospitality skill can't be measured by sales alone.

- **Hostesses with many regular nominations** → Strong at building a loyal customer base
- **Hostesses with many in-store nominations** → Exceptional at first-impression hospitality
- **Hostesses with high repeat rates** → Able to build lasting relationships

These strengths accumulate naturally as POS data. They become the hostess's own "career assets." Looking further ahead, we also want to visualize contribution during assist shifts — the ability to support the entire venue through teamwork.

When moving to a new club, you can demonstrate your strengths with data. The receiving venue can also use data to determine "this person fits our style." Not gut feeling — matching based on actual track records.

**Flip it around: if you work at a store without Luna Pos, none of this proof gets recorded.** You might have strong customer relationships, but you have no way to show that to your next employer.
